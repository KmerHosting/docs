A KmerHosting VPS is built for teams that need full control over the server environment. It fits APIs, internal tools, Docker-based apps, custom web stacks, and workloads that do not fit the boundaries of shared hosting.

## Choose a VPS when you need

- Root or sudo-level access.
- Custom packages or operating-system level changes.
- Node.js, Python, Docker, Redis, queues, or WebSockets.
- Reverse proxies, firewalls, and process supervision you manage directly.
- Isolated resources and a clearer operational boundary.

## Customer responsibilities

On a VPS, the customer is responsible for the server unless a managed-service agreement says otherwise.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Secure SSH access">
    Prefer SSH keys, remove unnecessary users, and avoid leaving password-only access exposed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restrict the firewall">
    Open only the ports the workload actually needs, usually `22`, `80`, and `443`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Patch the system">
    Keep the operating system, runtime, and package managers updated on a regular schedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Back up the service">
    Protect both application files and databases, then verify restore steps before the first incident.
  </Step>
</Steps>
